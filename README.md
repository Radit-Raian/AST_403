**AST403: Galaxies and Intergalactic Medium** explores the structure, dynamics, and evolution of galaxies, as well as the properties of the intergalactic medium (IGM). This course covers both theoretical concepts and observational techniques, including:

- Milky Way structure and kinematics  
- Galaxy morphology and classification (spiral, elliptical, irregular)  
- Galaxy photometry, spectroscopy, and population synthesis  
- Central supermassive black holes and AGNs (Active Galactic Nuclei)  
- Galaxy groups, clusters, and large-scale structure  
- Intergalactic absorption systems and baryon acoustic oscillations  
- Observational surveys like SDSS, 2dF, and DESI
